Global Electritcity Consumption

Electricity Consumption is an amount of electricity being consumed by the population, countries and all the world. It has been an important energy that the human uses it since after bulbs were created by Thomas Edison. The electricity consumption can be generated from many different sources such as coal, diesel, gas, water and other energy sources. There are two sources to generate the electricity. One is Non-renewable energy sources and another one is renewable energy sources.

World Total Electricity Consumption until 2016

It visualizes the total electricity consumption around the world. In this visualization, there is color hue used for the color choice. When the country consumes less electricity, then the color will be in light green. Likewise, the higher the electricity consumption, the darker the color it has such as from yellow to dark red. In this idiom, there is high electricity consumption in United States, Canada and Australia. Let's explore why these country have higher electricity consumption than other countries.


Renewable Energy Production around the world

Renewable Energy is important in Earth as it is energy derived from natural resources. It produces electricity with less damage impact on environment compared to non-renewable energy. It can reduce the greenhouse gas emissions. This map idiom visualises the renewable energy production around the world. Hence, it is important to explore the renewable energy sources around the world. Renewable Energy included solar electricity, hydro electricity, wind electricity and other renewable electricity. In this visualisation, countries like Canada, Norway & Sweden is the world leaders that generates the highest renewable energy. Likewise, most countries in Africa does not much rely on the renewable energy. This is because the highly cost of implementing renewable energy technologies in their countries.
